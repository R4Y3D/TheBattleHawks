The Bateleur

Status: Threatened

By: Frankie Kimball

Description
- The bateleur is an eagle native to areas of Africa and the Middle East.
- They are tree-nesting birds that spend most of the day (9 hours) searching for prey.
- It is most recognized by its red face and feet, with black and white plumage.
- From the ground, it is also recognized for its acrobatic flying.
- Its name means "performer" in French.
- It is a well-known bird in Africa and is represented on Zimbabwe's national emblem.
